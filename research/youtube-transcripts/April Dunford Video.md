# April Dunford Video Research

Video Title:
April Dunford: The Positioning Mistakes That Kill B2B Deals

Video Link:
https://youtu.be/SHB2Ols_YdY?si=CGGaX-TkEGfwieuo

## Key Insights:

* Most lost B2B deals are not caused by poor sales execution but by weak positioning that fails to clearly differentiate the product.
* The biggest competitor in enterprise software is often the customer's preference for maintaining the status quo rather than choosing another vendor.
* Category creation is frequently overrated; positioning within an existing category usually helps customers understand value faster.
* Effective positioning focuses on the deal champion first, since they are responsible for driving internal consensus and purchase decisions.
* Positioning should be treated as an ongoing strategic process that evolves with market conditions, customer priorities, and competitive dynamics.

## Why This Matters:

Many B2B SaaS companies invest heavily in demand generation, sales enablement, and product development while overlooking the fundamental role of positioning. April Dunford explains that when prospects cannot clearly understand why a product is different, valuable, and low-risk, they often choose not to buy at all. Strong positioning reduces indecision, shortens sales cycles, improves win rates, and helps companies communicate value in a way that resonates with real customer needs rather than internal assumptions.

## Top 3 Takeaways:

1. **Your biggest competitor is often "no decision"** — If prospects do not clearly understand why change is necessary, they will choose the status quo.
2. **Position for the champion** — Build messaging around the person driving the purchase internally, then address other stakeholders through objection handling.
3. **Continuously audit positioning** — Market conditions, competitors, and customer perceptions evolve, making positioning a living strategy rather than a one-time exercise.
